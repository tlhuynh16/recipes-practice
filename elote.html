Elote

Description

Elote is a Mexican street food that consists of corn on the cob covered in a creamy chili lime sauce. The origins of this popular dish trace back to the Aztecs. In fact, the term elote comes from the Nahuatl word for corn.

Ingredients

  • 4 ears of corn
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/2 cup cotija cheese
  • 1 lime, quartered
  • Hot sauce
  • Chili powder

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Shuck the corn, remove the silk and stalk. Add corn to water, cover and cook for 5 minutes, then remove.
  2. When corn is cool enough to touch but still warm, insert wooden stick into corn.
  3. Brush corn with 1 tablespoon of mayonnaise, sprinkle with 2 tablespoons of cheese a squeeze of lime wedge. Add hot sauce and chili powder to taste.
  4. Repeat with remaining ears of corn and serve.
